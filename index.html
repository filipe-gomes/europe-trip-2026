Day 1 — Aug 16 (full day)

Belém, Tram 28 & the Oceanarium
  • Morning — Belém district: Jerónimos Monastery first (opens 10 AM, fill up by noon — go early). The Manueline stone carvings look like something from a fantasy novel; kids are genuinely awed. Combined ticket with Belém Tower ~€16/adult, under 12 free.
  • Belém Tower: 10-min walk from the monastery along the river. Iconic 16th-century fortress — climb the narrow tower for Tagus river views.
  • Lunch: Pastéis de Belém bakery — the original custard tart, made since 1837. Join the queue; it moves fast and it's worth every minute. Get a table inside and order coffee.
  • Afternoon — Tram 28: Catch it at the Figueira stop and ride through the winding cobblestone alleys of Alfama up to the Miradouro da Graça viewpoint. One of the great urban experiences in Europe — tight, loud, hot in August, and the kids will love every second.
  • Late afternoon — Lisbon Oceanarium (Parque das Nações): One of the best aquariums in Europe — a massive central ocean tank visible from four sides. Your 5-year-old will be completely transfixed. Pre-book online; ~€20/adult, ~€15/child.
  • Evening: Dinner in Parque das Nações neighbourhood (modern, walkable, less touristy than Alfama) or take the metro back to Baixa-Chiado for dinner in the Príncipe Real area.

Day 2 — Aug 17 (full day)

Sintra day trip (highly recommended) + Fado evening
  • Morning — Sintra day trip: 40-min train from Rossio Station (central Lisbon). Pre-book Pena Palace timed entry now at parquesdesintra.pt — this is non-negotiable in August, it sells out weeks ahead. The palace looks like a Disney castle painted in yellow and red, sitting on a hilltop in mist. Kids react as if they've walked into a fairy tale. Allow 2 hours at Pena Palace.
  • Moorish Castle (free, 20-min walk from Pena): Ancient battlements with views over the Atlantic. Your 10-year-old will scramble up the towers; your 5-year-old will need a hand on the steep paths but will love the drama of it.
  • Lunch in Sintra town: The village at the bottom has good restaurants — try Casa Piriquita for travesseiros (puff pastry rolls) or Incomum by Luis Santos for proper Portuguese lunch.
  • Return to Lisbon by 4–5 PM by train from Sintra station.
  • Evening — Fado dinner: Book a restaurant with live fado performance — Casa de Linhares (Alfama, slightly more theatrical) or Clube de Fado (more intimate, musicians are excellent). Both welcome families; kids are generally captivated by the raw emotion of fado even without understanding the words. Book well in advance for August.
If Sintra feels like too much given the early departure next morning, swap it for a relaxed Cascais day (40-min coastal train from Cais do Sodré) — beautiful beach town, kids swim, you have a seafood lunch by the ocean, home by 4 PM for an early night.

What to do with ~3 hours in Madrid

⭐ Primary stop — both ages love this
🌳 Parque del Buen Retiro
Metro: Retiro station (Line 2) · Free entry · UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2021
Madrid's most beloved park — 125 hectares of formal gardens, shaded promenades, fountains, and a large artificial lake. Perfect for two kids who've been on planes since 5:45 AM and need to run around. This is exactly where locals come with children on a Tuesday morning and the atmosphere is relaxed and genuine.
  • Estanque Grande del Retiro (the lake): Rent a rowing boat for 45 minutes (~€6/boat, fit 3 people). Your 10-year-old can take an oar. The Alfonso XII monument across the water is spectacular. Kids universally love this.
  • Palacio de Cristal (Crystal Palace): A stunning Victorian glass-and-iron pavilion built in 1887, currently used as a contemporary art exhibition space. Free to enter. Even if there's no interesting exhibition, the building itself is extraordinary — kids react to the scale and the light.
  • Playgrounds: Several throughout the park, well-maintained. Your 5-year-old gets to run free after the morning flight.
  • Monument to the Fallen Angel: Near the Rose Garden — the only public monument in the world dedicated to the devil (Lucifer's fall from heaven). Your 10-year-old will find this delightfully strange. It sits exactly 666 metres above sea level — tell them that and watch the reaction.
  • Street performers and musicians: The main promenades always have buskers and performers in August mornings. Kids stop and watch.
Buy breakfast inside the park at one of the outdoor kiosks — tostada con tomate (bread rubbed with tomato and olive oil) and café con leche is the quintessential Madrid morning. €3–4 per person. The kids will want churros — there's a kiosk near the lake.
Optional — if energy allows
🎨 Prado Museum (exterior + quick visit)
5-min walk from Retiro Park's western edge · Children under 18 always free
The Prado is right next to Retiro — if your 10-year-old has any interest in art or history, a focused 45-minute visit is doable. Children under 18 enter free. Don't try to see everything; go directly to Velázquez's Las Meninas (Room 12) — arguably the most famous painting in Spain, and genuinely mind-bending even for children who aren't museum people. Then Goya's Saturn Devouring His Son (Room 67) — your 10-year-old will not forget it.

Skip the Prado entirely if the kids are tired or cranky — the park is the real star of this layover and you shouldn't feel obligated.
🍽️ Lunch in Retiro before heading back
  • El Brillante (just outside the Prado, Glorieta del Emperador Carlos V) — legendary Madrid bar famous for its bocadillo de calamares (squid sandwich). A Madrid institution. Kids who like seafood will love it; even the bread alone is excellent.
  • Restaurante Jardines del Retiro (inside the park) — sit-down terrace restaurant. Pricier but lovely setting. Good for a proper lunch if you want to stay in the park.
  • Park kiosks — sandwiches, drinks, ice cream. Fast and easy, keeps you in the park longer.
⏰ Be back at the metro by 2:05 PM at the latest. The journey from Retiro to Terminal 2 takes about 25 minutes. You want to be through security and at your gate well before 3:45 PM (when check-in closes).

Aug 19 — Paris city day

🗼 Paris with kids — the best one-day itinerary
9:00 AM – 12:00 PM
Eiffel Tower + Trocadéro
  • Book timed entry NOW at book.toureiffel.paris — August tickets sell out 4–6 weeks in advance. The 2nd floor is enough for kids and has shorter queues than the summit; the view is just as spectacular. Aim for the 9:00 or 9:30 AM entry slot to beat the crowds and the heat.
  • Trocadéro Gardens: Walk across the Pont d'Iéna after your tower visit and climb the Trocadéro steps for the classic postcard view with the tower behind you. The fountains at Trocadéro are running in August — your 5-year-old will want to paddle.
  • The walk from Orly to the tower is ~30 min by metro (RER B to Antony, then Line 6 to Bir-Hakeim) or ~25 min by Uber/taxi. With two kids and a morning start, a taxi is worth the ~€20.
12:00 PM – 2:00 PM
Lunch + Jardin du Luxembourg
  • Lunch: Head to Saint-Germain-des-Prés. Grab a crêpe from a street vendor on Rue de Buci, or sit down at Café de Flore or Les Deux Magots (pricier, but the setting is iconic and the croque-monsieur is excellent for kids).
  • Jardin du Luxembourg: 10-min walk from Saint-Germain. One of the best park experiences in Paris for families — formal French gardens with a large basin where children rent miniature wooden sailboats and push them with long sticks (€4, your 5-year-old will do this for 45 minutes happily). There's also a Théâtre des Marionnettes (puppet theatre, performances in French but universally understood by children), a carousel, swings, and a sandbox. Free to enter the park.
2:30 PM – 5:00 PM
Seine walk + Notre-Dame
  • Seine walk: Stroll east from the Luxembourg area towards the river and walk along the Left Bank towards Notre-Dame (~2 km, completely flat, beautiful). Cross onto Île de la Cité.
  • Notre-Dame de Paris: The cathedral's exterior restoration is complete and it's once again one of the most beautiful facades in Europe. The interior reopened in 2024 — check current access before visiting. Even from the outside, the Gothic architecture is extraordinary and kids respond to the scale.
  • Île Saint-Louis: The tiny island behind Notre-Dame has one main street lined with independent shops and the legendary Berthillon ice cream. Get two scoops; it's the best ice cream in Paris. End the afternoon here.
Evening
Early dinner + early bed before Disney
Tomorrow is Day 1 at Disneyland — you need to be at the park by 8:30 AM for Extra Magic Time (if staying Disney hotel) or 8:45 AM for general rope drop. Get dinner by 7 PM and kids in bed by 9 PM. A simple dinner near your friend's place — pizza, pasta, or a local brasserie — is perfect. Don't try to squeeze in another Paris landmark tonight.

Aug 20, 21, 22 — 3 days at Disneyland Paris

⚠️ August = peak crowds. Strategy matters.
August at Disneyland Paris — what to expect
August is the park's busiest month. The new World of Frozen (opened March 2026 in Disney Adventure World) is driving record summer attendance. With 3 days, you have the luxury of a relaxed pace — you don't need to rush or stress. The key is rope drop discipline on the first two days.
Three-day strategy: Day 1 = Disneyland Park (the castle park), hit the biggest rides early. Day 2 = Disney Adventure World (World of Frozen + new areas), rope drop here. Day 3 = relaxed revisit of both, character meets, shows, anything missed. Three days means you can enjoy the evening shows every night — Disney Illuminations (castle light show) is the undisputed highlight of the whole resort.
Day 1 — Aug 20 · Disneyland Park (castle park)
The "wow" day — iconic rides and the evening show
  • Arrive by 8:30–8:45 AM (park opens 9 AM in August). Be through security and at the gates early. The first 90 minutes are genuinely magical — short queues, cool morning air, half the crowd still in their hotels.
  • Rope drop order: Big Thunder Mountain first (102 cm min height — your 10-year-old is in; check your 5-year-old) → Peter Pan's Flight (both ages, always the longest queue in the park, must do early) → Phantom Manor (haunted house, both ages love it, genuinely atmospheric).
  • Mid-morning: Pirates of the Caribbean (always air-conditioned, lower wait in late morning, both ages). Buzz Lightyear Laser Blast (interactive shooter ride, your 10-year-old will be competitive about the score).
  • For the 5-year-old — Fantasyland: Dumbo the Flying Elephant, Mad Hatter's Tea Cups, the Carousel, It's a Small World (if open). This area is perfect for little ones and rarely has long waits.
  • Midday break (1–3 PM): Critical with a 5-year-old in August heat. Return to your friend's if close enough, or find a shaded sit-down restaurant inside the park. Pirates of the Caribbean has an attached air-conditioned restaurant (Blue Lagoon) — book in advance for a magical setting right on the water where the boats float past.
  • Afternoon: La Tanière du Dragon — the dragon sleeping underneath Sleeping Beauty Castle. Free, no queue, eerie and spectacular. Your kids will talk about this all day.
  • Evening: Disney Stars on Parade (~5:30 PM, check app). Then stay for Disney Illuminations — the castle light show runs around 10:30–11 PM in August. This is the single best thing at Disneyland Paris. Position yourselves slightly back from the hub (not pressed against the front railing) for the full view. Both kids will remember this forever.
Day 2 — Aug 21 · Disney Adventure World
World of Frozen + new Disney Adventure World
Disney Adventure World was completely relaunched on March 29, 2026 with World of Frozen, a new Tangled ride, Adventure Bay, and a large lake with its own nighttime spectacular. This is the most exciting new development at Disneyland Paris in years. Go here on Day 2 when everyone is energised after a rest night.
  • Rope drop (arrive 8:30 AM): Go directly to Frozen Ever After — the brand-new headline ride set in Arendelle. This will have the longest queue in the park all summer. Hit it within the first 10 minutes of opening.
  • Next: The new Tangled-inspired ride (debuted March 2026) → Ratatouille: L'Aventure Totalement Toquée de Rémy — a fantastically fun ride through the Ratatouille movie. Both kids will adore it. Book Premier Access (~€15) if the queue is already building.
  • Avengers Assemble: Flight Force: For your 10-year-old — the best roller coaster at the resort. Surprisingly short wait given how good it is.
  • Crush's Coaster (Finding Nemo): spinning coaster, 107 cm min height. Check if your 5-year-old qualifies. Your 10-year-old will want to ride it twice.
  • A Celebration in Arendelle: Outdoor Frozen show by the new fjord lake. Beautiful, emotional, great for both ages. Check the app for show times.
  • Evening lake show: The new nighttime spectacular on Disney Adventure World's lake runs at the same time as Disney Illuminations in Disneyland Park. Since you saw Illuminations last night, watch the lake show tonight — brand new in 2026.
Day 3 — Aug 22 · Relaxed revisit + character meets
The leisurely day — no stress, re-ride favourites
With three days, Day 3 is gift — no must-do agenda. Re-ride whatever your kids loved most, take time for character meet-and-greets (which require patience that you don't have on rope-drop days), see Mickey and the Magician indoor show, explore areas you walked past, and soak in the atmosphere.
  • Mickey and the Magician (Disneyland Park, indoor show): Excellent stage show with classic Disney characters and real magic effects. Air-conditioned — perfect for the hottest part of the afternoon. Genuinely impressive for both ages.
  • Character meet-and-greets: Mickey, Stitch, and the Princess Pavilion all use virtual queues on the Disneyland Paris app. Open the app 5 minutes before the virtual queue launches; use mobile data rather than park Wi-Fi (mobile is faster). Get your picks before 9:30 AM or they'll be gone.
  • Re-ride your kids' favourites from Days 1 and 2. Queues are often shorter on weekday mornings — Aug 22 is a Saturday, so arrive early to beat the local weekend crowd surge.
  • Disney Illuminations one final time — different spot tonight, same magic. It never gets old.
Getting to Disneyland from Orly: RER B from Orly (via Antony or CDG connection) to Marne-la-Vallée–Chessy — right at the park gates. ~50–60 min. Or an Uber/taxi from Orly is ~50 min and €60–80 for the family — worth it on rope-drop mornings when you need to arrive at a precise time.

Day 1 — Aug 24
Ribeira, Dom Luís Bridge & Port wine lodges
  • Morning — Ribeira waterfront: The medieval riverside district, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Walk along the quay, watch the traditional rabelo boats on the Douro, eat pastéis de bacalhau (salt cod pastries) from a street vendor. The colourful medieval buildings stacked up the hillside behind you are exactly what you imagined Portugal to look like.
  • Dom Luís I Bridge walk: Climb to the upper deck (pedestrian walkway) for the best panoramic views over Porto and the Douro river valley. Completely free, slightly exposed — your 10-year-old will love it; manageable for the 5-year-old with a hand held on the narrow parts.
  • Vila Nova de Gaia — Port wine lodges: Cross the bridge to the south bank. Take the cable car (~€6/person each way) down from the bridge for spectacular views. Tour Graham's or Sandeman's port wine lodge (~€15/adult, children often free) — the cellars are cool, fascinating, and the guides are excellent. Your kids get fresh grape juice while you taste.
  • Clérigos Tower: 225 steps up the baroque bell tower for panoramic views over Porto's rooftops. €8/adult, €4/child. Worth the climb.
  • Evening dinner: Cantina 32 (Rua das Flores 32) — superb Portuguese petiscos (sharing plates) in a relaxed, atmospheric setting. Book ahead.
Day 2 — Aug 25
Livraria Lello, São Bento & Matosinhos beach
  • Livraria Lello: One of the most beautiful bookshops in the world — a neo-Gothic interior with a famous curved red staircase. Pre-book entry tickets online (€8, redeemable against any purchase). Your kids will be genuinely awestruck.
  • São Bento Station: 5-min walk from Lello. The station concourse is covered in 20,000 azulejo tiles depicting Portuguese history. Free to enter. One of the most beautiful train stations in Europe — don't miss it.
  • Matosinhos beach: 20 min by metro (Line A to Matosinhos Sul). Porto's best local beach — sandy, broad, and the seafood restaurants on Rua Heróis de França directly behind the beach are excellent. Lunch at A Gavina or Marisqueira Matosinhos: grilled whole fish, chips, cold Vinho Verde. Perfect family lunch after a morning of culture.
  • Optional — Serralves Museum: Contemporary art museum with enormous, gorgeous gardens full of outdoor sculptures and lawns. Kids love exploring the grounds. €20/adult, under 12 free. Good for a late afternoon wander before dinner.
  • Pack tonight for the early drive south tomorrow.

Aug 26 — The drive south: Porto → coast → overnight

Full driving day — ~5 hours driving + stops
🚗 Porto → Óbidos → Sintra → Restaurante da Adraga → Nazaré overnight
Pick up rental car at Porto Airport (OPO) · ~300 km total today
Stop 1: Óbidos — 1h 45min from Porto
A perfectly preserved medieval walled village — walk through the main arch gate into cobblestone streets, whitewashed houses with yellow and blue trim, and a hilltop castle. Kids feel like they've walked into a fairy tale. Buy ginjinha (sour cherry liqueur) served in an edible chocolate cup from one of the street-level window bars — you sip, the kids eat the chocolate cup. Spend 1.5–2 hours, then continue south.
Stop 2: Sintra — 1 hour from Óbidos (optional)
If you did Sintra on Aug 17 from Lisbon, skip it today and drive directly to Adraga. If you didn't do Sintra yet, stop here — pre-book Pena Palace timed entry at parquesdesintra.pt. Arrive by 11 AM, allow 2 hours at the palace, leave by 1 PM before the crowds peak. The drive from Sintra to Restaurante da Adraga takes only 15 minutes.
🎯 The centrepiece stop — book ahead now
🌊 Restaurante da Adraga — lunch on a wild Atlantic beach
Rua da Praia da Adraga, Colares · 15 min west of Sintra · Established 1905 · ☎ +351 219 280 028
A family-run seafood restaurant sitting directly on one of Portugal's most dramatic wild beaches — golden sand flanked by black volcanic cliffs, massive Atlantic surf, and views of the Sintra hills. The drive down the valley to the beach is already stunning.

Must-order: Polvo grelhado (grilled octopus — the standout dish), dourada grelhada (grilled sea bream presented whole at the table before cooking), and the Pavlova dessert. Kids can have simple grilled fish with chips — they will eat it all.

⚠️ CRITICAL: Pre-reserve by phone now. They fill up weeks ahead in August. Aim for a 1:00–2:00 PM lunch slot. Call +351 219 280 028 or email them directly.
After lunch: spend an hour on Praia da Adraga. The water is cold Atlantic (~18°C) with powerful waves — good for rock pool exploring and paddling, not serious swimming with a 5-year-old. The clifftop walk north towards Praia Grande takes 20 min and has spectacular views.
Overnight stop
🌊 Overnight in Nazaré — 45 min from Adraga
Or Peniche (30 min from Adraga) for a grittier, more authentic fishing-port feel
Nazaré is the standout choice: famous for the world's biggest waves (at Praia do Norte — even in August you can watch surfers from the clifftop Sítio viewpoint above town). The main family beach (Praia da Nazaré) in the bay below is calmer and perfect for swimming. The town has a funicular, dried fish hanging from every doorway, and a genuine Portuguese character that feels nothing like a resort. Hotel Mar Bravo is right on the seafront and solid for families.

Peniche is a working fishing port — smaller, fewer tourists, outstanding seafood straight off the boats. Either works perfectly as a one-night stop.

Where to stay — Lagos is the best family base

⭐ Best Algarve pick for ages 5 & 10
Stay in Lagos — 2 nights (Aug 27–28)
Why Lagos: The most beautiful beaches in the Algarve (Praia Dona Ana, Praia do Camilo — sheltered golden coves with dramatic cliffs), a charming authentic old town, and the world-famous Ponta da Piedade sea caves. Less of a resort, more of a real Portuguese town. Perfect for your age mix.

For your 5-year-old: Meia Praia (5 min east of town) — long, flat, shallow, completely calm. Ideal.
For your 10-year-old: The Ponta da Piedade kayak tour through sea caves — one of the best experiences in Portugal for this age.
🏖️ Beaches around Lagos
  • Praia Dona Ana — sheltered golden cove 5 min from town, dramatic rock arches. Arrive early (by 9 AM) in August to get a good spot.
  • Praia do Camilo — stairs down a cliff face to a smaller, more secluded cove. Gorgeous. Calmer water, less crowded than Dona Ana.
  • Meia Praia — 4 km of flat sandy beach east of the marina. Shallow, calm water, beach bars. Best for the 5-year-old.
  • Praia de Luz (20 min west) — calm bay, village atmosphere, excellent restaurants behind the beach. Top family beach in the western Algarve.
🚣 Must-do activities in Lagos
  • Ponta da Piedade kayak tour (2–3 hrs): Paddle through golden sea caves, arches, and grottos at Portugal's most photogenic coastal spot. Your 10-year-old paddles their own kayak; your 5-year-old sits in a double with you. Kayak Lagos offers excellent family tours (~€40/adult, €25/child). Book at least 1–2 weeks ahead — sells out in August.
  • Boat tour from Lagos Marina: 1–2 hr tour through the same sea caves by motor boat. More relaxed option if the kayak feels too ambitious for your 5-year-old that day (~€20/adult, €10/child).
  • Lagos old town: Walk the medieval town walls, visit the Mercado de Escravos (the first slave market in Europe — sobering history, thoughtfully explained for older kids), and the Igreja de Santo António with its extraordinary gold-gilded interior.
  • Slide & Splash waterpark (near Lagoa, 30 min east): The best waterpark in the Algarve. Your 10-year-old will spend 3 hours on the speed slides and Black Hole ride; your 5-year-old has a dedicated shallow play zone. Great for a scorching August afternoon (~€35/adult, €25/child).
